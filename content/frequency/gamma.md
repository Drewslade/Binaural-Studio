Gamma refers to fast oscillations above roughly 30 Hz. In neuroscience it's discussed in connection with binding together perception across brain regions, attention, and working memory — and it's also the band where measurement is hardest and hype runs furthest ahead of evidence.

## What people use it for

Gamma sessions are popular in "peak performance" circles, usually around a 40 Hz beat. The honest summary: the research on *auditory* gamma entrainment for cognitive benefit in healthy adults is sparse and early. Some interesting work exists on 40 Hz sensory stimulation in laboratory settings, but it's a long way from "listen to this and think faster."

## In the studio

- The studio's beat range tops out at **30 Hz** — the doorway of gamma
- If you want to experiment at the top of the range, keep sessions short and the volume low
- Expect texture more than transformation: at these rates the beat becomes a fast flutter

## Worth knowing

We deliberately don't make gamma promises. If a future study meaningfully changes the picture, it'll show up on the research page first — that's the deal.
