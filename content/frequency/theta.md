Theta rhythms show up in the twilight states: the minutes before you fall asleep, deep relaxation, hypnagogic drift, and — in experienced meditators — sustained meditation. Frontal midline theta in particular has been linked to focused internal attention, which is part of why the meditation community is so attached to this band.

## What people use it for

Theta-range beats (4–8 Hz) are the go-to for meditation sessions, body-scan practices, and pre-sleep wind-downs. A 6 Hz beat is one of the most-studied frequencies in the small research literature on binaural beats, with some EEG studies reporting increased theta activity during listening.

## In the studio

- Set the beat between **4–8 Hz** (the Meditate preset uses 6 Hz)
- Try a mid-low carrier around **130–180 Hz**
- The **Theta Trance** journey ramps from 9 Hz down to 4.5 Hz and back over 12 minutes
- Brown noise pairs well with this range

## Worth knowing

Feeling drifty during a theta session doesn't necessarily mean your brain is "in theta" — relaxation, expectation, and simply sitting still with your eyes closed all produce the same feeling. Enjoy the state; hold the explanation loosely.
