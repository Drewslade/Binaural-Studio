## The setup

Sleep sessions live at the slow end: a beat between **2–4 Hz**, a low soft carrier (100–200 Hz), and brown noise doing most of the audible work. The Sleep preset (3 Hz) is the direct route. The **Hypnagogic Dip** journey is built specifically for the transition: it starts at a wakeful 10 Hz, drifts down to 6 Hz across the middle of the session, and holds there while you (ideally) stop being awake to notice.

## How to use it

1. Volume matters more here than anywhere else — set it barely above audible
2. Use the WAV export to render a session and play it from a sleep-friendly device, rather than keeping a bright screen by the bed
3. Isochronic mode works over a room speaker if wearing headphones in bed is miserable (it usually is)
4. Same time, same audio, most nights — consistency is the actual active ingredient

## What to actually expect

Audio can make the runway to sleep more comfortable: it masks noise, occupies a restless mind, and signals that the day is over. What it can't do is override stress, caffeine, or a disrupted schedule — and persistent insomnia deserves a clinician, not a longer playlist. Treat this as sleep *hygiene*, not sleep *medicine*.
