## The setup

For work sessions, the common recipe is a beat in the **14–18 Hz** range — squarely in beta — with a carrier tone around 200–250 Hz and a quiet layer of pink noise underneath. In the studio, the Focus preset (15 Hz) gets you there in one tap, or run the **Focus Ramp** journey: it starts at 10 Hz, climbs to 18 Hz over five minutes, and holds there for a full 25-minute block before easing off.

## How to use it

1. Put on headphones (binaural mode needs them — the beat literally doesn't exist without stereo separation)
2. Start the audio *before* you open the work, not after — let it mark the boundary
3. Keep the volume low enough that you forget it's there within a minute
4. Work the block, stop the audio, take a real break

## What to actually expect

Three things are happening at once, and only one of them is controversial:

- **Masking** — steady audio covers unpredictable noise, which measurably helps concentration
- **Ritual** — a consistent start-of-work cue trains your brain the way a gym playlist does
- **Entrainment** — the possible nudging of brain rhythms toward the beat frequency, where the evidence is genuinely mixed

If binaural beats help you focus, some blend of all three is why. We think that's a perfectly good deal.
