## The setup

This is the simplest session on the site: the Relax preset, which is a **10 Hz** beat on a 220 Hz carrier — the studio's defaults, untouched. Ten hertz sits in the middle of the alpha band, the rhythm most associated with calm, eyes-closed wakefulness, and it's the frequency that shows up most often in the relaxation and anxiety studies.

## How to use it

1. Twenty minutes, headphones, somewhere you can close your eyes
2. Add pink noise if a bare tone feels too sterile
3. Nothing to do, nothing to count — this one is allowed to be passive
4. Good pairing for a post-work decompression or a pre-difficult-conversation reset

## What to actually expect

Anxiety and relaxation outcomes are where the binaural beat literature is least embarrassing: several controlled studies, including a pre-operative anxiety trial, reported modest but real reductions compared to control audio. Modest is the key word — this is a pleasant nudge toward calm, not a sedative. But a reliable, zero-cost pleasant nudge is nothing to sneeze at.
