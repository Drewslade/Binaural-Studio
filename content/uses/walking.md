## The setup

For walking, most people land in **alpha (8–12 Hz)** — settled but awake — or slide toward theta for slower, more contemplative walks. A 10 Hz beat with a hint of pink noise makes a surprisingly good walking companion: it's featureless enough to fade into the background and steady enough to discourage the podcast-brain habit of filling every walk with words.

## How to use it

1. **Safety first, genuinely:** keep the volume low enough to hear traffic, or use one earbud, and skip it entirely near roads and bikes
2. Export a 30–60 minute WAV from the studio so your phone stays in your pocket
3. Let the walk set the pace and the audio set the mood, not the reverse
4. Works best on routes you already know — novelty and theta don't mix

## What to actually expect

There's no walking-specific binaural beat research to cite, and we won't pretend otherwise. What you're getting is a screen-free, lyric-free alternative to music that keeps some attention available for the world — a structured way to be alone with your head for an hour. People have been walking to think for a few thousand years; this is just a quiet metronome for it.
